Question D: What Prince George's County Residents Need to Know

What Is Question D?

A question on the Novermber 2016 ballot to create two new "At-Large" Council Seats that are exempt from the current term limits.

Why We're Against Question D

It costs one million dollars a year to support two new council members

The At-large seats will be effectively owned by developers and wealthy special intrest groups because only they can afford to fund At-large campains

It will allow current country council members to bypass term limits

Who Is Behind Question D?

Council members whose term limits are about to expire and wealthy development corporations who aren't satisfied with the current political situation

How did this happen?

Question D was placed on the ballot despite public outcry and after a hearing in which only former council members and developers testified for the question.

The Washington Post called Question D "a jobs plan for Prince George's County council members"
